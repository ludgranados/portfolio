Happiness & Wellbeing

L.A.G Blog

Happiness

Happiness is the term used in regards to the mental or to the emotional state of an individual. It includes pleasant and/or positive emotions that range from being content to achieving an intense joy. The term happiness is also used for an individual’s life satisfaction.

Wellbeing

Wellbeing refers to the higher-level condition of an individual. With wellbeing one’s condition is positively increased.

Happiness can be increased by placing ones focus on things that one can be grateful for. By focusing on the good things that exist in your life a conscious focus is taking place that may provide you with emotional as well as interpersonal benefits. When gratitude is cultivated the appreciation of the things that are going good in your life can help to create a sense of abundance, of joy of having good things occur in your life. Being happier makes a contribution to a person’s wellbeing which can be the outcome of cultivating the general attitude. Cultivating a general attitude of gratitude can help to reach a state of mind that allows one to appreciate not only major events, but also the simple things in life. Never under estimate the little because a little can go a long way. Just as a cup of coffee may seem a simple pleasure that may brighten up your day, so can the small things that have been good in your day. At one point of life people often begin to hear about counting their blessings. It may be beneficial to an individual to once in a while sit down and do so. In the current fast paced world we live in, the emotion is not only at times neglected but also underestimated as a virtue. Cicero (a Roman philosopher) once declared that “gratitude is not only the greatest of the virtues but the parent of all others”. Gratitude shows us high moral standards that allow an individual to choose to celebrate life. To give to ones self a lesser sense of disappointment, anger, hate, sadness, or fear. Rather then an exchange to offset a burden, gratitude is a gift. It is a gift that one is able to give to his or herself.
