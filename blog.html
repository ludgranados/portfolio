A Little goes a Long Way

There are no small decisions that may not contain a step towards a change. When we make a choice we signal our brain that we are ready to begin what ever journey the day may present us with. Believing that their are no options may lead to giving up or shutting down, while believing in the existence of options may create an opportunity that may not have existed before. The matrix had a choice of taking the red pill or the blue pill. Even the choice of making a choice is the decision being made by the individual. Every decision has a consequence which can be both good or bad. Habits can be altered by an individual by his or her choices being made throughout the day. A threatened brain is different from a safe feeling brain. Threatened by anxiety of new experiences, or by previous experiences feeling without any other option other then failure, or the safety of familiarity or of knowing the existence of options to make things ok. Every person has faced the world differently and decisions being made have had an impact in their lives one way or another. Something as small as skipping lunch or choosing the wrong meal can have an impact on that person. Eating healthy can lead to one thing and eating fast food can lead to a different road. Just like putting on a shoe leads to putting on the other, choices are made all day and the simplest choice may change or alter a behavior. Habits become old but begin somewhere somehow.
